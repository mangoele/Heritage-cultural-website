Traditional Sounds & Movements

Music and dance are central to Basotho cultural life. They connect people during celebrations, rituals, and storytelling. Explore the instruments and dances that bring our traditions to life.

Traditional Instruments of the Basotho

Lesiba

A unique string-wind instrument made from a quill and string, producing humming tones. Traditionally used by herders.

Phalaphala

A ceremonial horn made from animal horn, blown to call meetings or signal rituals.

Lekhubu

A wooden flute used in traditional ceremonies and herding songs.

Mololi

A reed whistle used by children and herders for play and communication.

Kudu Horn

A deep-sounding horn used in rituals and processions.

Thomo

A musical bow with one string, played by striking or plucking while resonating with the mouth.

Setolo

A plucked string instrument resembling a traditional lute, handmade by local craftsmen.

Likopelo

A bow-like string instrument producing rhythmic background tones.

Kora (adapted)

A harp-lute with 21 strings, adapted into Basotho ceremonial music.

Mokorotlo Drum

A hide-covered drum played in community dances and the famous Mokorotlo hat dance.

Maropa

A set of tuned drums producing layered rhythms during performances.

Ntlong

A clay pot drum used mainly by women in initiation songs.

Moropa

A large ceremonial drum used in war dances and royal gatherings.

Teboho

A handheld drum accompanying praise poetry and storytelling.

Lehlaka Rattle

Seed-filled rattles tied to dancersâ€™ ankles or hands to add rhythm.

Ditlhaka

Bamboo percussion sticks struck together for rhythm.

Kalimba

A thumb piano with metal keys producing melodic tones.

Likhesho

Seed pod shakers worn around the waist or legs for dance rhythm.

Setoloho

A wooden slit drum used for communication between villages.

Mokhele Bell

A metal bell marking rhythm in ceremonies and dances.

Lekolulo

A vocal-whistle instrument producing overtone sounds, often with chanting.

Hlohoea

A mouth harp producing buzzing tones, resonated by the mouth cavity.

Liphala Voices

Vocal horns or imitations of horn calls created using the human voice.
